Twitter to RSS

  • So what is this?

    SpySatellite is a service for converting Twitter streams into Atom feeds. Supports user tweets, tweets for a certain hashtag, as well as search results.
    It is written in Python, using Flask and Beautiful Soup. The code is free and open-source and is available on GitHub.

  • Doesn't this sort of thing already exist?

    You are right, there already are several services doing this. Unfortunately, none of them work particularly well. The only one that worked for me at all was TwitRSS.me, but even it can't correctly handle a lot of stuff, uncluding quotes, multiple images, emoji, and line breaks. They motivate this behaviour by the intention to keep compatible with the v1 API, which I would probably have a bigger attachement to had I arrived at the Twitter RSS scene a few years ago, but alas.

  • Is there anything that your service can't do?

    Polls are not supported, at least not yet. SoundCloud frames are not handled, though YouTube ones are.
    Twitter media, in the form of videos and fat JavaScript frames Twitter calls "gifs" for some bizzare reason, are also not shown.

  • What about replies?

    Not shown by default, but you can enable them by appending /with_replies to the feed url. Note that self-replies don't count as replies and are shown by default.

  • What are the limits of the service?

    Feeds are cached for 17 minutes, as of right now. Rate limit is 6 requests per minute, after the burst of 50 requests. Should be more than enough for now.
    May be changed later, obviously.
